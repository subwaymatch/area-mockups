# Sketchfab community 3D models of phones & laptops — catalog

**Date:** 2026-07-09
**Scope:** community-made 3D models of popular consumer devices on [Sketchfab](https://sketchfab.com) — free **and** premium — with brand, model, link, license, price, formats and complexity. Every link below was fetched and verified live during research; dead or ambiguous listings (broken pages, `license: null`, unconfirmed store status) were dropped rather than listed.

> Context: these are candidates for composing richer react-three-fiber scenes around `area-mockups` devices (glTF/GLB loads via drei's `useGLTF`; [`gltfjsx`](https://github.com/pmndrs/gltfjsx) converts models to typed JSX). The mockup devices themselves stay procedural — no downloads needed.

## How Sketchfab licensing & buying works now (verified June–July 2026)

- **Free downloads** carry a license the uploader picks: a Creative Commons variant (CC0, CC BY, CC BY-SA, CC BY-NC, CC BY-ND…) or Sketchfab's own non-CC **"Free Standard"** license (no attribution required). The exact license is shown on each model page — it varies per upload, so check every model individually.
- **Paid models** grant Sketchfab's **Standard License**, or **Editorial License** where flagged (editorial-only: no commercial/promotional use, no implied brand endorsement).
- **Sketchfab → Fab migration:** Sketchfab (Epic-owned) still runs as its own site for viewing and free CC downloads, but its Store closed on Oct 22, 2024 when Epic launched **[Fab](https://fab.com)** (successor to the Sketchfab Store, Unreal Marketplace, ArtStation Marketplace and Quixel). Paid model pages on sketchfab.com now show a **"Get it on Fab"** button instead of a price — the actual transaction (and current price) lives on the parallel `fab.com` listing. Free-model URLs are stable and self-contained.
- **Trademark caveat (applies to every entry below):** the license — CC or Standard — covers only the *artist's* copyright (mesh, textures, UVs). Sketchfab's Standard License states explicitly that it grants **no rights** to "names, people, trademarks, trade dress, logos or registered, unregistered or copyrighted designs" depicted in the model. A CC-licensed iPhone model does not license Apple's trade dress. For commercial marketing material, unbranded/generic devices (like this package's procedural models) remain the safe route; realistic branded replicas warrant legal review.

## Observations

- **Free ≠ uniform:** most free device models are CC BY 4.0 (attribution required), but outliers exist in both directions — Sketchfab's own "Free Standard" (no attribution needed) and CC BY-**NC** (noncommercial only, e.g. the mistjs Galaxy S25 Ultra). Always read the license line on the model page.
- **Paid models mostly hide their price on Sketchfab** since the Store moved to Fab — expect "Get it on Fab" and a price on fab.com (typically $10–30 for a single device based on sellers who still print prices in titles, e.g. iSteven's $15 MacBooks).
- **Sellers relabel listings in place** to track Apple's cycle (M3 → M5 in the same URL), so a link's slug may not match the advertised generation. Check the live title.
- **Poly counts span 5k → 600k+** — for web/react-three-fiber use, prefer the sub-100k models or decimate before shipping; a 600k-tri hero model is fine for a single showcase, heavy for a page of mockups.
- **Formats:** free downloads always offer Sketchfab's auto-converted glTF/GLB/USDZ regardless of source format, which is exactly what drei's `useGLTF` wants. Store purchases ship the seller's source formats (often FBX/Blender/Max) that may need conversion.
- Dropped during verification: a madMIX "iPhone 17 collection" stub (degenerate 4-triangle page), a dika3d iPhone 17 Pro with `license: null`, a JNO_Models iPhone 17 with ambiguous store status, and one dead Galaxy S24 link.
