`<TVSet>` and `<TVSetMockup>` render a modern flat-screen (65" by default,
any diagonal from 32" to 98" via `size`): a near-bezel-less 16:9 panel with
a subtle off-state gloss, thin edges with a shallow electronics bulge low
on the back, a recessed rear input bay (HDMI ×3, USB ×2, LAN, optical
audio, antenna coax) and two slim feet near the ends — each a pair of
wide-splayed struts, the shallow Λ stance of current retail stands
(referenced from LG UT8000 / Samsung DU7200-class product photos). The
screen is a live 1920×1080 DOM surface — full desktop layouts, showreels,
and `<video>` all apply.

Sizing follows real product ranges rather than uniform scaling: the panel
scales with the diagonal, while the bezels, cabinet depth, ports and feet
keep hardware dimensions — the feet hold a near-constant inset from the
panel ends and grow only mildly across the range.

## Props

Beyond the [shared screen props](/docs/screen-content):

| Prop | Type | Default | Description |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| `size` | `number` | `65` | Diagonal in inches, clamped to 32–98. Panel scales; bezels, ports and feet follow real product ratios. |
| `color` | `string` | `'#15171b'` | Enclosure colorway (frame, back, feet). |
| `resolution` | `number` | `1920` | Virtual display width; 1920 gives 1920×1080. |

There is no `orientation` prop; it lives on a media stand.
