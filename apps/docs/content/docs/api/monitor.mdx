`<Monitor>` and `<MonitorMockup>` render an Apple Studio Display style 27"
monitor (the March 2026 second generation — the 2022 enclosure carried over
unchanged), proportioned from the official product photography: ~19.5 mm
all-aluminum slab with an edge-to-edge glass front (uniform ~13 mm black
bezel, hairline aluminum rim), the barely-there centered camera, the
gloss-black Apple mark on the upper back, the tight rear cluster of
2× Thunderbolt 5 + 2× USB-C slots, the captive power cord's circular recess
centered low on the back, speaker perforations along the bottom edge, and
the signature tilt stand — a ~148 mm thin-sheet blade curling over its
hinge barrel (polished pivot caps on the sides) with the single vertical
stadium cutout punched clean through: the round power inlet shows through
its upper half and the captive cord's open drop through the lower — plus
(faithfully) no power button. The mockup grounds the stand on a virtual
desk plane.

## Props

Beyond the [shared screen props](/docs/screen-content):

| Prop | Type | Default | Description |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| `colorway` | `string` | – | Retail colorway id from `MONITOR_COLORWAYS` (`silver`). Explicit `color` overrides it. |
| `color` | `string` | `'#c8cbd0'` | Aluminum colorway (enclosure and stand). |
| `resolution` | `number` | `2560` | Virtual display width; 2560 gives 2560×1440, the Studio Display's logical desktop, so real desktop layouts apply. |

There is no `orientation` prop; it lives on a desk.
