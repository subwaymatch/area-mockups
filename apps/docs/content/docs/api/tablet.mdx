`<Tablet>` and `<TabletMockup>` cover the current Apple iPad lineup — iPad Pro
(M5), iPad Air (M4) and the standard iPad (A16) — plus the Samsung Galaxy Tab
S11 family, at true relative sizes. Each variant gets its real hardware: the
Pro's camera pod with LiDAR and flash, the Air's and iPad's bare single lens
with the Touch ID top button, the Apple Pencil strip and back Smart Connector
dots; protruding camera rings, quad speaker slots and keyboard pogo contacts
on the Galaxy Tabs, plus the Ultra's U-shaped display notch. Every back
carries its brand mark as real vector geometry — the Apple glyph centered,
the SAMSUNG wordmark along the edge exactly like the print — and every
non-notch tablet has its front camera in the bezel on the landscape-top edge.

The virtual display swaps to 1376×1032 — real desktop-class layouts apply.

## Props

Beyond the [shared screen props](/docs/screen-content):

| Prop | Type | Default | Description |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| `variant` | `'ipadpro13' \| 'ipadpro11' \| 'ipadair13' \| 'ipadair11' \| 'ipad11' \| 'tabs11' \| 'tabs11ultra'` | `'ipadpro13'` | Which tablet: iPad Pro 13"/11" (camera pod, Pencil strip), iPad Air 13"/11" (single lens, Touch ID), iPad A16 (single lens, edge Smart Connector), Galaxy Tab S11 (single camera ring, pogo contacts) or Tab S11 Ultra (dual rings and the U-shaped notch). |
| `orientation` | `'portrait' \| 'landscape'` | `'portrait'` | Landscape lays the tablet on its side and swaps the virtual display (for example 1376×1032 on the 13" iPad) with upright content. |
| `resolution` | `number` | per variant | Defaults to the variant's logical grid: 1032×1376 (ipadpro13), 834×1210 (ipadpro11), 1024×1366 (ipadair13), 820×1180 (ipadair11, ipad11), 800×1280 (tabs11), 924×1480 (tabs11ultra). |
| `colorway` | `string` | first entry | A retail colorway id from `TABLET_COLORWAYS` — e.g. iPad Pro `spaceblack`/`silver`, iPad Air `spacegray`/`starlight`/`purple`/`blue`, iPad `silver`/`blue`/`pink`/`yellow`, Galaxy Tab `gray`/`silver`. |
| `color` | `string` | per colorway | Any explicit body color, overriding the colorway. |
