Every device is procedurally generated at runtime from real product research:
no GLB files, no hosting, no pop-in. Variants within a family keep true
relative sizes because they share one world scale. Non-device mockups (books,
brochures, billboards, a cargo van, …) live on the
[Objects and print surfaces](/docs/objects) page.

**Resolution basis.** The Galaxy S26 (and the foldables' FHD+ panels) are
their panels at one-third scale. The QHD+ S26 Ultra uses One UI's default FHD+
render at 450 dpi. Galaxy Tabs use their xhdpi dp grid (panel at half scale,
an estimate since Samsung does not publish logical grids). iPhones, iPads, the
Watch, the MacBooks, and the Studio Display use their exact point grids
(2x or 3x).

## Orientation

Pass `orientation="landscape"` on any phone or tablet to lay the device on its
side. The virtual viewport swaps to the landscape resolution and your content
renders upright, exactly like rotating the real device. Hardware details ride
along: the Tab S11 Ultra's droplet notch, for example, sits on the right edge
in portrait and top center in landscape, matching the physical device.

## Renderer-agnostic specs

The raw device dimensions are exported as plain data: `GALAXY_VARIANTS`,
`IPHONE_VARIANTS`, `FOLD_VARIANTS`, `FLIP_VARIANTS`, `LAPTOP_VARIANTS`,
`TABLET_VARIANTS`, `PHONE`, `IPHONE`, `LAPTOP`, `TABLET`, `WATCH`, and
`MONITOR`. The same numbers will drive the planned 2D (CSS/SVG) renderers.
