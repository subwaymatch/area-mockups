---
title: Introduction
description: Interactive, GPU-accelerated 3D device mockups for React, with real live DOM on every screen.
---

`area-mockups` renders procedurally generated 3D devices with WebGL and maps
real, live DOM onto their screens. Buttons click, videos play, iframes scroll.
There are no 3D asset files to host and nothing to download at runtime.

## Highlights

- **Sixteen devices**: the full Galaxy S25 family (S25, S25+, S25 Ultra, S25 Edge),
  the full iPhone 17 family (17, 17 Air, 17 Pro, 17 Pro Max), a MacBook Air 13"
  (M5), iPad Pro 13" and 11" (M5), Galaxy Tab S11 and S11 Ultra, an Apple Watch
  Series 11 and Galaxy Watch 8 wearing full wristbands, and a Studio Display
  style 27" monitor.
- **Eleven objects**: hardcover book, magazine, tri-fold brochure, business
  card (front and back), ID badge on a lanyard, gallery poster frame, retail
  product box, roll-up banner stand, 14'×48' billboard, a city transit bus
  with a king-size ad, and a wrap-ready cargo van — the printable surface of
  each is a live DOM area.
- **True-to-device screens**: each virtual display defaults to the real device's
  logical resolution in portrait and landscape, so your layouts and breakpoints
  behave exactly like on the hardware.
- **Real GPU rendering**: three.js and react-three-fiber, physically based
  materials, studio lighting, soft shadows, clamped device pixel ratio.
- **Any content on screen**: pass React components, an `<iframe>`, or a
  `<video>` as children. Pointer events, state, and scrolling keep working.
- **Composable**: use the one-liner mockup components, or drop the bare devices
  into your own react-three-fiber scene.

## Installation

```bash
npm install area-mockups three @react-three/fiber @react-three/drei
```

React 18 or newer (19 recommended). `three`, `@react-three/fiber`, and
`@react-three/drei` are peer dependencies.
